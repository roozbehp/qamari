# The Shawwal of 1378 AH is very confusing in how the newspaper handles it:
# First, on 1959-04-09, it announces that the Shawwal crescent has been seen by religious authorities and thus 1378/10/1 AH=Thu 1959-04-09.
# In the same issue, it says that this is different from what calendar experts had computed,
# and interviews Abbas Riazi Kermani, a calendar authority who says it would have been impossible to see the crescent.
# The newspaper follows the reported observed crescent by religious authorities with renumbered Shawwal dates until 1378/10/7 AH=Wed 1959-04-15
# (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Ettelaat13380125.pdf)
# It then switches to the precomputed calendar on 1378/10/7 AH=Thu 1959-04-16 (https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Ettelaat13380126.pdf),
# and keeps it that way going forward!
# It's very hard to decide which way to go for this month, especially because the precomputed calendar had both Ramadan and Shawwal as 30 days,
# which means moving the beginning of Shawwal one day early would make it 31 days!
# The solution would have been moving the beginning Dhu al-Qa'dah one day early too, but it seems that that hadn't happened,
# at least not according to the newspaper.
# To be safe, we are commenting out Dhu al-Qa'dah and will try to find other sources,
# since we don't want to make Shawwal of 1378 AH thirty-one days.
